Rajshahi at a glance

Rajshahi is a major city located in northwestern Bangladesh. It is the capital of the Rajshahi Division and one of the largest cities in the country. Rajshahi is known for its historical, cultural, and educational significance. Here are some key aspects of Rajshahi: Geography and Location: Rajshahi is situated in the northwestern part of Bangladesh, near the border with India. The city is strategically located on the banks of the Padma River, one of the major rivers in Bangladesh. Educational Hub: Rajshahi is often referred to as the "Education City" of Bangladesh due to its numerous educational institutions. Rajshahi University (University of Rajshahi) is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the country. The city is also home to other colleges, schools, and institutions. Agriculture: The region around Rajshahi is agriculturally productive, known for its mango orchards, silk production, and rice cultivation. The city is often called the "Silk City" due to its historical association with silk trade and production. Historical and Cultural Heritage: Rajshahi has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was part of various empires and dynasties, including the Pala Empire and the Mughal Empire. Some notable historical sites in and around Rajshahi include the Bagha Mosque, Puthia Temple Complex, and the Varendra Research Museum. Mango Capital: Rajshahi is famous for its mangoes, especially the prized "Langra" and "Alphonso" varieties. The city hosts the annual Rajshahi Mango Festival, celebrating the region's mango cultivation. Tourism: The city and its surrounding region offer various tourist attractions, including the Padma River, Varendra Research Museum, and Mahasthangarh, an ancient archaeological site. The Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world, is also relatively accessible from Rajshahi. Transportation: Rajshahi is well-connected by road, rail, and air. The city has its own airport, Shah Makhdum Airport, which connects it to Dhaka and other major cities in Bangladesh. Economy: Agriculture, trade, and education are significant contributors to the local economy. The city serves as a commercial center for the surrounding agricultural region, including the trade of crops like mangoes, silk, and rice. Population: As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, Rajshahi had an estimated population of around 700,000 people. However, population figures may have changed since then. Rajshahi is known for its historical and cultural heritage, educational institutions, and agricultural production. It is a place where tradition and modernity coexist, making it an important city in the northwestern part of Bangladesh.
