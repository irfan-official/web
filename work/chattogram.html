Chittagong at a glance

Chittagong, also known as Chattogram, is a major city and seaport located in southeastern Bangladesh. It is the second-largest city in the country after Dhaka, the capital. Chittagong serves as a vital economic and commercial hub for Bangladesh due to its strategic location on the Bay of Bengal. Here are some key aspects of Chittagong: Geography and Location: Chittagong is situated on the southeastern coast of Bangladesh, near the border with Myanmar (Burma). The city is known for its picturesque natural surroundings, including hills, rivers, and the coastline. Economic Significance: Chittagong is home to the country's busiest seaport, the Port of Chittagong, which handles a significant portion of Bangladesh's international trade. The city's economy is driven by industries such as shipping, manufacturing, and commerce. Industries: The Chittagong region has a diverse industrial base, including shipbuilding, textiles, steel, and chemicals. The Chittagong Export Processing Zone (CEPZ) has attracted foreign investment and plays a crucial role in the export-oriented manufacturing sector. Tourism: Chittagong and its surrounding areas are known for their natural beauty and tourist attractions. Some popular destinations include Cox's Bazar, which boasts the world's longest natural sea beach, and the hill stations of Bandarban and Rangamati. The region is also known for its vibrant culture and ethnic diversity. Culture: Chittagong has a rich cultural heritage influenced by various ethnic groups, including Chakma, Marma, and Tripura communities. The city hosts various cultural festivals, music events, and traditional dance performances. Education and Institutions: Chittagong is home to several universities and educational institutions, including Chittagong University, Chittagong University of Engineering and Technology (CUET), and Chittagong Medical College. Transportation: In addition to its seaport, Chittagong is well-connected by road, air, and rail. Shah Amanat International Airport serves as the city's primary airport, providing domestic and international flights. History: Chittagong has a long and storied history, with archaeological evidence suggesting ancient trade links with the Roman Empire and other civilizations. It has been ruled by various dynasties and empires, including the Maurya, Gupta, and Mughal Empires. The city played a significant role in the struggle for independence during the Bangladesh Liberation War in 1971. Population: As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, the population of Chittagong was estimated to be over 2.5 million people. However, please note that population figures may have changed since then. Chittagong is a dynamic and bustling city that contributes significantly to Bangladesh's economy and cultural diversity. Its strategic location, vibrant industries, and natural beauty make it an important and attractive destination in the country.
